# ADR 0002: Position TEMPO in Work & Productivity

- Status: accepted
- Date: 2026-07-17
- Decision owner: human business owner

## Context

The initial implementation brief selected Developer Tools by default. The human
business owner subsequently selected OpenAI Build Week's **Work & Productivity**
track and requested explicit alignment with the official judging criteria.

The same deterministic kernel can be described narrowly as a developer control
or broadly as an operating workflow. The commercial problem is cross-functional:
teams waste research, product, design, and engineering effort when an agent starts
building before the decision, evidence, budget, success threshold, and kill
condition are explicit.

## Decision

1. The submission category and product narrative are Work & Productivity.
2. The primary audience is founders, product managers, innovation teams,
   agencies, and AI-enabled delivery teams. AI engineers are an important user,
   but not the only buyer or beneficiary.
3. The D0 journey must visibly shorten a real team workflow:
   `business question → evidence gap → cheapest experiment → charter → human
   authorization → bounded build → verification receipt → human verdict`.
4. Technical gates remain the proof, not the headline. The product experience
   must make blockers actionable, show why work is stopped, and produce a memo a
   business owner can use.
5. Official judging criteria are equally weighted. We therefore optimize for:
   a non-trivial deterministic implementation; a coherent runnable journey;
   a specific credible impact case without fabricated savings; and a clearly
   differentiated upstream-governance thesis.

## Judging-panel alignment boundary

The official criteria and host tips are evidence. Individual judges' private
tastes are not published. Product choices that emphasize concise flows,
progressive disclosure, visible evidence, safe defaults, and restrained visual
design are informed design judgments, not claims about unpublished panel
preferences.
